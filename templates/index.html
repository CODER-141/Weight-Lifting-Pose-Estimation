💪 Biceps Curl Workout

Target Muscles: Biceps Brachii, Brachialis

To ensure proper form and reduce the risk of injury during arm exercises:

✅ Keep your elbow angle between 40° and 165° at all times.

🔁 This range helps maintain tension on the muscles while protecting your joints.

🚫 Avoid fully locking out (180°) or collapsing below 40°, as these positions can strain your elbows.

📌 Keep your elbows close to your body — don't let them swing forward.

🎯 Focus on slow, controlled movements within the safe angle zone.

🏋️ Squat Workout

Target Muscles: Quadriceps, Glutes, Hamstrings, Core

To ensure proper form and reduce the risk of knee and back injuries:

✅ Keep your knee angle between 70° and 160° during the movement.

📌 Stand with feet shoulder-width apart, toes slightly pointed outward.

🔁 Lower your body by bending knees and hips together — not just the knees.

🚫 Avoid letting your knees cave inward or go past your toes excessively.

⬇️ Squat until your thighs are parallel to the floor (knee angle ~90°).

🎯 Keep your chest up and back straight throughout the movement.

🙌 Overhead Press Workout

Target Muscles: Deltoids (Shoulders), Triceps, Upper Chest

To ensure proper form and avoid shoulder injuries:

✅ Keep your elbow angle between 70° and 180° during the press.

📌 Start with the bar or dumbbells at shoulder height, elbows slightly in front.

🔁 Press the weight straight up over your head until arms are fully extended.

🚫 Avoid arching your lower back — keep your core tight and engaged.

⬆️ At the top, your ears should be in front of your arms.

🎯 Lower the weight slowly and in a controlled manner back to shoulder level.

🏗️ Deadlift Workout

Target Muscles: Hamstrings, Glutes, Lower Back, Traps, Core

The deadlift is a powerful full-body movement. Proper form is critical to avoid injury:

✅ Keep your back flat (neutral spine) throughout the entire lift.

📌 Stand with feet hip-width apart, bar directly over mid-foot.

🔁 Hinge at the hips first, then bend the knees — not the other way around.

🚫 Never round your lower back — this is the most common cause of injury.

💪 Drive through your heels and push the floor away as you lift.

🎯 Keep the bar close to your body — it should almost drag up your shins.

🚣 Barbell Row Workout

Target Muscles: Lats, Rhomboids, Traps, Rear Deltoids, Biceps

To build a strong back safely with proper rowing technique:

✅ Keep your elbow angle between 50° and 160° during the row.

📌 Hinge forward at the hips, keep your back flat and parallel to the floor.

🔁 Pull the bar into your lower chest / upper abdomen, not your neck.

🚫 Avoid using your lower back to jerk the weight — use your back muscles.

⬇️ Lower the bar in a slow, controlled motion to full arm extension.

🎯 Squeeze your shoulder blades together at the top of each rep.
